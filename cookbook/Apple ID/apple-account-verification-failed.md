# Apple Account Verification Failed

Customer cannot complete Apple Account or iCloud sign-in because verification fails, the code does not arrive, or the device cannot connect to Apple account services.

---

## Symptoms

- "Apple ID verification failed."
- "It says cannot connect to server."
- "I never get the verification code."
- "The code is sent to an old number."
- "iCloud keeps asking me to sign in."
- "Apple账号一直验证失败。"

---

## Possible Causes

1. **Network path cannot reach Apple services**
   - Very common with captive Wi-Fi, VPN, DNS filtering, cellular data disabled for Settings, or unstable network.
2. **Two-factor authentication code cannot be received**
   - Trusted phone number, trusted device, or notification delivery is unavailable.
3. **Apple Account email or password problem**
   - Wrong password, locked account, or email verification issue blocks sign-in.
4. **Account recovery is required**
   - If the customer cannot access trusted numbers or devices, normal in-store troubleshooting cannot bypass recovery.
5. **System date, software, or service state issue**
   - Incorrect date/time, old system software, or temporary Apple service issues can block verification.

---

## Official Apple Solution

Verification: Official

Steps:

1. Test on a known-good network before changing account settings.
2. On iPhone or iPad, confirm cellular data is allowed if the customer is signing in away from Wi-Fi.
3. Request a verification code from a trusted device or trusted phone number.
4. If email verification is the issue, resend the verification email from the Apple Account website.
5. If the account is locked, disabled, or the password is unknown, follow Apple Account recovery guidance.
6. Explain that Apple cannot speed up account recovery once it starts.

## Community Verified Workarounds

Unofficial

### Switch networks before retrying sign-in

- Source: Repeated Retail and community troubleshooting pattern.
- Success probability: High when error says server, connection, or verification failed.
- Risks: Low.
- Notes: Use cellular or a trusted Wi-Fi network. Avoid captive portals and VPNs during account sign-in.
- Verification: Verified

### Disable VPN or filtering temporarily

- Source: Repeated reports across Apple account and iCloud sign-in issues.
- Success probability: Medium.
- Risks: Low if temporary and customer understands why.
- Notes: Do not remove enterprise security profiles without authorization.
- Verification: Likely

## Retail Troubleshooting Flow

1. Ask the exact message: **verification failed**, **code not received**, **password issue**, or **account locked**.
2. If it says connection or server, test network first.
3. If it asks for a code, identify whether the customer has access to any trusted device or trusted phone number.
4. If email verification is missing, resend the email and check spam or mailbox access.
5. If trusted access is unavailable, move to account recovery. Do not promise immediate access.
6. Stop device troubleshooting when the issue is clearly account recovery or locked-account state.

---

## Escalation

Contact Apple Support when:

- The account is locked, disabled, not active, or requires recovery.
- The customer cannot access trusted numbers or trusted devices.
- Billing, purchase, or account security questions are involved.

Visit Apple Store or repair provider when:

- The device cannot connect to Wi-Fi or cellular outside Apple Account sign-in.

Repair or replace hardware when:

- Network hardware failure is confirmed by broader Wi-Fi or cellular diagnostics.
